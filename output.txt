--- Methodology ---
This aggressively optimized clustering solution implements a purely unsupervised NLP pipeline built rigorously from scratch. Adhering to the hackathon's strict constraints, NO pre-trained models, embeddings, or transformer pipelines (e.g., BERT, Word2Vec, GloVe) were used. The architecture relies on an advanced custom text processing subroutine that normalizes text, strips punctuation, and applies a 'Nuclear' Stopword Expansion (removing pervasive meta-dataset boilerplate). Once processed, a TfidfVectorizer derives semantic bigram structures using an aggressive N-Gram vectorization strategy (ngram_range=(1,2)). The matrix is heavily pruned (max_df=0.85, min_df=3) to focus precisely on the core concepts. Subsequently, dimensionality is radically condensed to a tight scope (n_components=20) using TruncatedSVD (LSA). This strips away high-frequency noise. Lastly, K-Means clustering algorithmically partitions this space, establishing optimal groupings via structural Silhouette Scores.

--- Proof of Contexts ---
The sampled cluster outputs starkly illustrate contextual optimization. By pruning meta-chatter and forcing the pipeline to analyze bigrams, terms sharing identical context windows organically gravitate together within an ultra-dense subset of LSA space. Our generated 'Reason' fields—mapped directly from tight TF-IDF array centroids—explicitly validate that the algorithm successfully bypasses superficial dataset descriptions, instead linking raw, contextual humor templates devoid of pretrained tools.
